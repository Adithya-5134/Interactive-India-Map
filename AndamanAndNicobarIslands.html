Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Barren Island, India’s Only Active Volcano

Quick Facts

  • Area: 8,249 Sq. Kms
  • Capital: Port Blair
  • Language: Hindi, English, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Nicobarese
  • Population: 3,79,944
  • Famous for: Pristine Beaches, Cellular Jail, Scuba Diving, Marine Life
  • Major Islands: Havelock Island, Ross Island, Neil Island, Great Nicobar

Importance of Andaman and Nicobar Islands

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are a group of 572 islands in the Bay of Bengal, known for their pristine beaches, rich marine biodiversity, and tribal heritage.

Port Blair, the capital, houses the infamous Cellular Jail, a historic symbol of India's freedom struggle.

The islands are an important strategic location for India, ensuring security in the Indian Ocean region.

Tourists visit the islands for their stunning coral reefs, scuba diving, snorkeling, and eco-tourism experiences.

The islands are home to indigenous tribes like the Sentinelese, Nicobarese, and Great Andamanese, who have lived here for centuries.

The region is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, protecting rare species like Dugongs (Sea Cows) and Leatherback Turtles.

With its eco-tourism, historical significance, and naval importance, the Andaman and Nicobar Islands are among India's most unique territories.

Historical Significance

The Cellular Jail in Port Blair, also called Kala Pani, was used by the British to imprison Indian freedom fighters.

The islands were occupied by the Japanese during World War II before being returned to India in 1945.

Tribal communities have lived on these islands for thousands of years, with little contact with the outside world.

Cultural Significance

The Nicobarese people have a distinct culture with unique folk dances and music.

Many festivals like Island Tourism Festival and Subhash Mela celebrate the region’s heritage.

The islands have a mix of Indian and Southeast Asian influences in their traditions and cuisine.

Tourist Attractions

  • Cellular Jail (Port Blair): A national memorial and historic site.
  • Havelock Island: Famous for Radhanagar Beach, one of Asia’s best beaches.
  • Ross Island: A former British settlement with colonial ruins.
  • Baratang Island: Known for its limestone caves and mud volcanoes.

Interesting Facts

  • The Sentinelese tribe is one of the world’s last uncontacted tribes, living in isolation on North Sentinel Island.
  • Radhanagar Beach in Havelock Island is ranked among Asia’s top beaches.
  • The islands are closer to Indonesia and Myanmar than to mainland India.
  • Dugongs, the state animal, are gentle marine creatures found in Andaman’s waters.
